## Installation

Get started with YouTube-Floater in just a few simple steps.

### macOS Installation

<Steps>
<Step title="Download the app">
  Visit [youtubefloater.com](https://youtubefloater.com) and download the latest version for macOS.
</Step>

<Step title="Install">
  1. Open the downloaded `.dmg` file
  2. Drag **YouTube-Floater** to your Applications folder
  3. Launch the app from Applications
</Step>

<Step title="First launch">
  On first launch, macOS may ask you to confirm opening the app since it was downloaded from the internet. Click **Open** to proceed.

  <Tip>If you encounter security warnings, go to System Preferences > Security & Privacy and click "Open Anyway"</Tip>
</Step>
</Steps>

## Using YouTube-Floater

Start watching YouTube videos in a floating window immediately.

### Basic Usage

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="link" title="Play a video">
    1. Copy any YouTube video URL from your browser
    2. Paste it into the YouTube-Floater URL input field
    3. The video will instantly load and start playing in the floating window
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="sliders" title="Adjust controls">
    Use the control panel to customize your viewing experience:
    - **Opacity Slider**: Adjust window transparency to see content underneath
    - **Volume Slider**: Control video volume independently from system audio
    - **Toggle Controls**: Hide/show the control panel to minimize distractions
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="arrows-up-down-left-right" title="Position and resize">
    - **Drag**: Click and drag anywhere on the window to reposition
    - **Resize**: Drag the window edges to adjust size
    - **Corner Snap**: Drag near screen corners to auto-snap the window
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="browser" title="Open in browser">
    Click the "Open in Browser" button to launch the current video in your default web browser.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## System Requirements

<Info>
  **Requirements**:
  - macOS 10.13 (High Sierra) or later
  - Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3) or Intel processor
  - Internet connection for video streaming
</Info>

## Tips & Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>

<Card title="Corner Positioning" icon="arrows-maximize">
  Drag the window to any corner for quick snapping - perfect for keeping videos accessible while working.
</Card>

<Card title="Transparency" icon="eye">
  Use higher transparency when you need to see content underneath, or lower transparency for focused viewing.
</Card>

<Card title="Keyboard Shortcuts" icon="keyboard">
  Standard YouTube shortcuts work - spacebar to pause/play, arrow keys to skip, etc.
</Card>

<Card title="Volume Control" icon="volume-high">
  Adjust volume independently - watch tutorials quietly while listening to music elsewhere.
</Card>

</CardGroup>

## Known Limitations

<Warning>
  YouTube implements CORS restrictions that may cause playback interruptions after several plays. If this occurs, simply refresh the video by re-pasting the URL.
</Warning>
